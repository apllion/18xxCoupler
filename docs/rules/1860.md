# 1860 — Rules Reminder

## Quick Reference
- **Players:** 2--4
- **Bank:** £10,000
- **Starting Cash:** £1,000 (2p) / £670 (3p) / £500 (4p)
- **Capitalization:** Full, 50% float
- **Shares:** 20% president + 8x10%
- **Sell Movement:** down per share
- **Sell After:** Any time (even before corp operates)
- **Unsold Dividends:** market -> corp
- **Pool Limit:** 100% (no limit)
- **Sell Order:** sell/buy
- **Must Sell in Blocks:** Yes
- **Currency:** pounds

## Specialties
- Isle of Wight setting ;(
- Receivership mechanics ;(
- Forced train buy from bank pool ;(
- 100% pool limit (all shares can be in market) ;(
- Southern Railway national forms at phase 9

## Share Structure
20% president + 8×10% (standard 10-share).

## Par Prices
Par from market column.

## Issue / Redeem
No issue/redeem. Full capitalization.

## Loans
No loans.

## Stock Market Movement
- **Pay dividend:** right 1 if >= price; right 2 if >= 2×; right 3 if >= 3×; right 4 if >= 4×
- **Withhold:** left 1
- **Sell shares:** down 1 per share sold
- **Sold out:** up 1 at end of SR

## Mergers
Southern Railway national forms at phase 9.

## Emergency Buy
No forced purchase. Trainless corps become insolvent and lease a notional train.

## Auction
English auction — ascending bids on each private.

## Round System
- SR -> OR x1 (phase 2)
- SR -> OR x2 (phases 3--4)
- SR -> OR x3 (phases 5--9)

## Game End
Bank break. Bankruptcy does not end game (receivership instead).

## Train Events
Rusting: 2→6, 3→8. Southern Railway forms at phase 9. Insolvency train leasing.
